How I work

From silhouette to shipped asset.

One pipeline, repeated for every piece. It's the same thinking whether the end product is a 16×16 sprite or a textured 3D model — and it's how I prove the whole 2D→3D flow actually holds together.

01

Concept & silhouette

Thumbnails, shape language and a strong silhouette first. If it reads as a flat shape, it'll read everywhere else.

PhotoshopKrita
02

2D — vector & pixel

Clean bézier line art and tight pixel sprites. This is where the design is locked: colour, proportion, and read at small sizes.

FigmaAseprite
03

3D — model, texture, light

Block out, retopo to clean low-poly, then texture and light. The 2D design is the reference, so the model never drifts off-model.

BlenderMaya
04

Ship — game-ready & composited

Optimise, rig where needed, drop into Unity, and grade or composite for the final frame. The asset arrives production-ready, not just pretty.
